    # PM Framing Quick Reference

    # Opening Frames

    • “The business problem we’re solving is…”
    • “This creates value for both customers and the business by…”
    • “Given T-Mobile’s competitive context…”

    # Decision-Making Framework

    • “We have three options: A, B, C. I recommend X because…”
    • “The tradeoff we’re making is [X] for [Y] because…”
    • “Our hypothesis is [X]. We’ll validate this by [Y].”

    # Risk/Mitigation Language

    • “The biggest risk is [X]. Our mitigation is [Y].”
    • “Early warning signals include…”
    • “If this assumption proves wrong, we’ll…”

    # Stakeholder Impact

    • “This impacts four groups: customers, agents, executives, compliance…”
    • “Win-win for all parties because…”
    • “From the user’s perspective…”

    # Metrics & Success

    • “Leading vs lagging indicators…”
    • “Success criteria are designed to…”
    • “North star metric is [X] because…”

    # Strategic Thinking

    • “This isn’t just customer service - it’s T-Mobile’s AI platform foundation”
    • “Our competitive advantage is…”
    • “From a platform perspective…”

    # Resource Optimization

    • “Given resource constraints and timeline pressure…”
    • “Optimizing for time-to-value means…”
    • “Make vs buy analysis shows…”

    # Scaling Philosophy

    • “Each phase de-risks different assumptions”
    • “We accelerate based on success, not timeline”
    • “The 0.5% to 50% path validates…”

    # Key PM Phrases

    • “Given the business context…”
    • “The business case hinges on…”
    • “This positions T-Mobile to…”
    • “User research would tell us…”
    • “The competitive dynamics suggest…”

    # When Challenged

    • “That’s exactly why we built in [safety mechanism]”
    • “The data will tell us if…”
    • “I’d rather [conservative approach] than [risky approach] because…”

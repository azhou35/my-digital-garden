    There are bees buzzing inside the cage of our own being.

    I hold an incredibly short memory. Dates and names pass through my fingers, my cheeks flush as I try to recount a story whose details I’ve already mangled. Sometimes I feel more sieve than sponge, information passing through me without absorption.

    Other days, I carry a deep vacuum. Therein lies a dense gravity that yearns to consume, something, anything. Is it knowledge? Is it distraction? It’s restless and endless.

    I’m pacing back and forth of the corridor of my mind looking for something that tells me I matter. It can’t come from anyone except me. I worry for the days I’m apart from you, I confess. I feel purposeless.

    Purposeless? You sound worried. Without aim, more like.

    In short time you’ve become adept at reading me. There are moments when I slip behind the veil, lips pursed to protect reality from the bees within me. I fear articulating my own anxieties and insecurities. I prefer to hold a seed of doubt in my mouth, chewing it like gum. Turn it over. Inside out. Speaking it into existence guarantees its embodiment.

    Or – it’ll pop. It could also reveal that my worries are all smoke, no body. A friend, self-branded fellow overthinker, once drew a diagram to describe what thinking felt like. This is the anxiety loop, she explained, pressing the ballpoint into paper into a dense circle of squiggles. Making art, writing, talking – she draws a straight line rightward from a point on the circle, then draws an arrow – helps us release the anxiety and stop the loop.

    The diagram reminds me of physics class. The problems where a track star spins around in a circle, gathering angular velocity. The moment of intense pressure from built-up anticipation before they let go. Arms collapse at sides and air caves in the middle, cleaved by the straight line of 10 pounds of a metal discus.

    Sometimes I feel like I’m on a hamster wheel. Running. Always running. I’ve ran an average of four miles every morning this year, for fear of what’ll happen when I stop. What happens when I let go?

    The days I come to you with pursued lips and endless runs on my mind – you instantly know when something is up. I clam up when upset and I am grateful you pluck out my anxieties, to be punctured & popped by you.

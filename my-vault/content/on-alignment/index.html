    “but it is important to note that curating your milieu isn’t only or even primarily about being picky. it is about how you relate to people.”

    im searhcing some platonic ideal of everything: food, people, passions.

    friendship is another dimension where resonance makes all the difference. in an coastal city it’s fairly easy to find people who are fuzzy matches to your personality, because the location attracts generally open-minded yuppies.

    but it takes time and intention to cultivate a close group of people who are aligned with your values - aka people who just get it. i find that the friends who stick share some common qualities: a shared notion of generosity, a sharp wit. it helps to have friends who like to host and are biased to hang, because some how the barrier to meeting up with people is this ability to just hang.

    when you find the right people, the relational energy between you creates emergent properties.

    # to become oneself

    i can’t do hard things. the prospect of 80-hour workdays in ib baffle me, the same way a cold plunge or 5 am wake ups do.

    i can’t do easy things, either. i can’t sit still and tang ping as much as i wish i could. i thrive at some messy middle: where it’s just challenging enough to feel rigor, but doable enough that i’m not discouraged.

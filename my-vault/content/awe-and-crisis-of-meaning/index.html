    why our capacity for awe is humanity’s most undervalued skill

    We ought to view ourselves with the same curiosity and openness with which we study a tree, the sky or a thought, because we too are linked to the entire universe.

    we live in a culture obsessed with personal branding & main character energy. yet, we are perhaps the only species that actively seeks the feeling of insignificance.

    a tree never contemplates its own majesty. an eagle doesn’t pause mid-flight to marvel at the vastness below.

    but humans? we climb mountains just to feel dwarfed by them; we stare at stars as reminders of our own cosmic irrelevance. we seek experiences that make us feel beautifully, purposefully small.

    this impulse has a name: the capacity for awe. and it’s the answer to our epidemic of self-importance and our crisis for meaning.

    awe invites a paradox. do you find this enlightening - a comforting feeling of interdependence - or terrifying - a dizzying reminder of insignificance?

    we have no control over when awe strikes but we can choose how we respond. we can either become terrified or empowered, retreating deeper within ourselves or move towards deeper understanding.

    awe is a lightning bolt that marks in memory those moments when the doors of perception are cleansed and we see with the startling clarity what is truly important life.

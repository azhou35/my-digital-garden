    # 2025 investment letter

    2025 was the year of performative males, bemoaning the persona who holds tote bags and drinks matcha. i found this trend quite peculiar - isn’t all a life a performance? i buy a polene purse to indicate quiet luxury.

    there’s this dual tension i hold as an asian girlie in tech. i want to differentiate myself and stand out, but deeply, earnestly, yearn to assimilate and be like everyone else.

    at raves when i don’t want to drink, i pop an edible and always feel removed from my body. i watch myself dance in third person, the lights streaking through a body that no longer belongs to mine.

    ai slop and ai-generated videos galore. where is the proof of presence here? i fail the twitter authentication gatekeeper at least 4 times and i question again what is my own personhood here.

    i think i fear being flattened, that i am not as interesting as i perceive myself to be.

    when i ask claude to do math it creates a program to compute the calculations. everything is just bits and bytes.

    all we can do today is to be authentic

    is prose personal or passive? do i live life or does it pass through me? is substack my truest voice or is it again a reflection of what i want to project into the world?

    i write towards a certain truth, which implies there is an objectivity.

    personal branding has exploded this year: tik tok girlies are picking up substack, companies are raising capital off of the promise of capturing attention. we dont need the metaverse to facilitate avatars - we are creating our own personas for the digital layer of the internet. at the same time, consumer brands have breached the personal - leveraging influencers and pop-ups to capture the hearts of america. 2 hour lines in soho just to get a free moisterizer.

    i hide behind metacommentary and relinquish my permission to feel.

    there’s this piece i return to, that always asks: what are you avoiding because it conflicts with some part of your identity / self-image?

    degrees of freedom

    there are certain anchors - mundane routines, like morning coffee. there are moments of peace and serenity and awe.

    as much humans, i live life in the extremes. i am fueled by the highs and the lows.

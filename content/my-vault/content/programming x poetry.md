#computationalpoetry
### "It’s a common misconception to think that programming and creativity are worlds apart. The assumption is grounded in a fundamental but incomplete truth...There is no room for imprecision in software. No place for double-meanings or subtext.” 
![[Pasted image 20240708101126.png]]
As Mark C. Merino describes, “code, especially mid- to high-level languages, exists not solely for computers, which could operate on machine language (essentially, representations of electronic signals), but for programmers as well.” How does our perception of code and code-writing change when we consider that our for-loops and classes and methods are being written not just for an unfeeling machine but for sentient, flawed humans?

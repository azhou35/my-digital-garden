**metaphor summer time memory**

we associate this season with new connections: the close friends you make at summer camps and internships, the situations that flame up and flare out. the days are longer, the humidity addles your inhibitions, so you spend borrowed time wholly invested in another soul. you climb mountains together, ride bikes, laze in the river.  

its in the early innings of a connection where you sense someone's natural boundaries and guards. we're really no different from delicate fruit - our insides are fleshy and messy, and wrap it under the emotional armor we show the world.  

some of us are raspberries, tender and exposed. ocean vuong's favorite fruit, so delicate they still need to be hand-harvested. some of us may be pomegranates, an impenetratable shell that harbors secret jewels inside. or consider the stone fruit, a deceivingly delicate disposition with a heart of stone when you dig in.

there's something i term as the permeability of connection: how much do we externalize to the outside work, and how much interiority do we expose? this builds on the idea of *intimacy thresholds*, [intimacy thresholds - by Nix ðŸ•Š - starting from nix ê©œ](https://www.startingfromnix.com/p/intimacy-thresholds) 

back then, i was wholly unaware of how much of myself i brought to a burgeoning connection. excited by the prospect of a new friend, i would overwhelm them with my lore - the breakup i was reeling from, the anxieties pressing into me about work and responsibilites, the deep yearning i had for something more. not too unlike a banana, over-ripening in the dark, until it bursts past its skin.  

under the guise of vulnerability there are old friends i will spill myself out to. or maybe, if not invited, the secrets will bubble out from me and reveal themselves in conversation. 

but at the end of the day, you have control over the intimacy you share. and maybe it's a power to diffuse this vulnerability with everyone i meet, and perhaps it's coincidence i meet so many people in the summer time.

**tension**

there is no proper way of tracking time in this season. there's this false promise of forever you can't control when the peaches ripen, where is the threshold between juices forming and flesh becoming overripe. 

there's no way to track the timeline of a friendship, when you are 100 days away from the last time you'll talk, 50, 25, 1.  every day is just a day until it's the last day before something ends. 

i remember the first summer away from home: summer camp in high school, how endless it felt, how alive the freedom and newness made me feel. 

but i never realized when would be my last summer at home. the summer before: dinners with my parents grilling in the backyard, bike rides around the block with neighbors, farmer market jaunts for the perfect jersey tomato. last time a place felt entirely mine. 

such is the unspoken cruelty of ripening. there's a familiar the ache to a sweetness arriving too soon, too late. we have these false expectations of perfect ripeness, but in reality the sweetness di perfectly ripe

summer days end in one of two ways:

either they grow hot and heavy with humidity until they explode under its own tension and pressure

or the hot rapidly dissipates and one day you wake up and it's the fall breeze agian. 

i'd imagine summertime connections are like this. 

i over-exert myself thinking about friendships and timing. that we're moving too fast, too slow, that i'm revealing too much and they too little. 

> I have always been scared of my own ripening

and maybe love is a willingness to peel each other's layers. some people grab you despite the mess of it all. family, close friends. my mom asks how im doing and i start crying. there are people who pierce your tough skin and see you for the fruit that you bear within. 

 hey. maybe connection isn't forever, maybe it isn't some promise of endless summer. maybe under a certain someone's gaze, you ripen a little too quickly. it's that cliche people say, *friends stay for a season or a reason,* that sometimes makes me wanna rip my heart out. 
 
 but what we can offer is there and now, the presence we can offer. 
 isn't grand gestures or promises of forever but the here and now, the presence we can offer. small plates of fruit, clementines exchanged at lunch time. 

forgive me, the mess i reveal of myself at ripening.

i have a funny theory that our relationship mirror fruits. 
every relationship is either a soft shell or hard shell fruit
* soft shell, soft inside - fuyu persimmons - burst at the seams
	* aka you make me feel so much and i feel so close to you i will literally overflow with conversation
	* raspberries - the most tender fruit that still needs to be hand-picked
* hard shell, soft inside - pomegranates
	* harder to crack, but when it opens its jolting and surprising 
	* bananas - also burst at the open, but the hard shell makes it mushy and leak out weirdly 
		* regardless of a hard shell, if my feelings are left to rot inside, they will inevitably spill out 
		* i am embarassed when i hold in my emotions too long and they inevitably ooze out

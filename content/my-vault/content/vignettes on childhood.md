back then your world fit neatly within four sides of a screen. suburbia is a faulty container for a child's imagination, but on the internet it could run free. 
you'd escape into digital worlds where you had complete control over your life: what your avatar looked like, the lands you explored. success was easily defined by the number of stars or trinkets or toy currency you'd accumulate. 

when i look back at child memories they're sepia-toned, frayed by years of neglect.

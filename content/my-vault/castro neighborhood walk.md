
Most residential buildings in the Castro were constructed between the 1880s and 1910s.

Dominant architectural styles:
* Victorian (Italianate, Queen Anne)
* Edwardian

Housing stock is primarily:
* 2–3 story, wood-frame buildings
* Narrow lots
* Bay windows, decorative trim, stoops, front stairs
* Wood construction helped many homes survive the 1906 earthquake and fire.

- Streets are:
    - Narrow
    - Walkable
    - Lined with residential facades facing sidewalks
        
- Common features:
    
    - Front doors and windows directly facing the street
    - Minimal front setbacks
    - Stoops and staircases creating semi-public thresholds
        
- Mix of:
    - Residential        
    - Small retail
    - Cafés and bars
